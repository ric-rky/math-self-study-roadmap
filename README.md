# Mathematics Self-Study Roadmap

A structured roadmap for self-studying mathematics through books, from foundational topics to advanced undergraduate and graduate-level mathematics.

This project was built based on my experience during my BSc and MSc in Mathematics. It focuses on books that are suitable for self-study, with a clear progression, study strategy, and transition criteria between phases.

## Live Version

- English: https://ric-rky.github.io/math-self-study-roadmap/
- Portuguese: https://ric-rky.github.io/math-self-study-roadmap/pt/

## Overview

The roadmap is divided into three tracks:

1. **Basic Track**  
   Foundations, pre-calculus, mathematical proofs, calculus, and real analysis.

2. **Undergraduate Track**  
   Core areas of a mathematics degree: analysis, algebra, geometry, and topology.

3. **Complete Track**  
   Advanced topics closer to graduate-level study and research.

## Why I Built This

I created this roadmap to help people who enjoy mathematics and want a long-term, book-based path for studying it seriously.

The goal is not to provide a shortcut, but to organize a realistic progression through books that are rigorous, coherent, and reasonably friendly for self-study.

## Notes on Language

Some references are Brazilian or Portuguese-language books. They are included intentionally, either because they are especially self-study friendly or because they played an important role in my own mathematical formation.

In future versions, I plan to include English-language alternatives for the main Portuguese references, making the roadmap more accessible to an international audience.

## Who This Is For

This roadmap may be useful for:

- self-learners who want to study mathematics through books;
- undergraduate students looking for a structured path;
- people coming from technical fields who want stronger mathematical foundations;
- anyone interested in learning mathematics with rigor and depth.
